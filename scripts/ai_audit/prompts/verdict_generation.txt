## Verdict request

Given the findings already drafted for {app_name}, produce the overall
verdict block that goes at the top of the report.

### Findings (already drafted, summary)

{findings_summary}

### Required output

* **label** (required, exactly one of the four below): pick the single
  label that best fits the finding mix. These are the only acceptable
  values:
    - "Ready to share" — no critical issues; the customer can send the
      live URL to a wider audience without embarrassment.
    - "Safe for friends/family testing" — minor polish only; OK for
      a private testing round with people who already root for them.
    - "Needs fixes before launch" — material issues; do not launch
      publicly until the criticals and highs are addressed.
    - "Do not invite real users yet" — severe issues (security,
      data isolation, broken auth, broken core flows); high risk if
      the URL is shared.

  Rough mapping (use judgment when a finding mix sits between two):
    - 1+ critical, especially security / auth / data isolation →
      "Do not invite real users yet"
    - 1+ critical that's a broken-flow or trust issue → "Needs fixes
      before launch"
    - no critical, 2+ high → "Needs fixes before launch"
    - no critical, 0-1 high, several medium → "Safe for friends/family
      testing"
    - no critical, no high (or only nits) → "Ready to share"

* **emoji** (required, derived from label): "🔴" if label is
  "Do not invite real users yet" or "Needs fixes before launch";
  "🟡" if label is "Safe for friends/family testing"; "🟢" if label is
  "Ready to share". The customer-facing PDF renders the label as the
  hero phrase and uses the emoji as a small accent.

* **summary** (required): a single sentence, 8-14 words, that explains
  *why* this label was chosen in plain English. Examples that pair
  with each label:
    - Ready to share: "No blockers; a couple of polish items to round
      it out."
    - Safe for friends/family testing: "Looks solid, just clean up the
      placeholder text before going wider."
    - Needs fixes before launch: "Two trust-page gaps and a broken
      booking flow to clear first."
    - Do not invite real users yet: "Dev login bypass on the live URL
      is the blocker."

* **narrative** (required): 3-5 sentences. If the app has genuine
  strengths visible in the evidence, name one specifically (e.g. "The
  onboarding flow is clear and the copy is clean"). If the app is
  genuinely broken or unfinished across the board, skip the positive
  opener and start with what the label is based on — forced positivity
  sounds worse than silence when the findings are severe. Then name the
  top one or two issues that drove the label, in plain language. Close
  with a forward-looking sentence on pacing the rest. Do not mention
  severities by code; talk about the fix in human terms.

### Voice rules

* No em-dashes. Use colons, periods, or two sentences instead.
* No "Certainly", "Overall", "In summary". Start with substance.
* Plain English. The customer reads this first; it sets the tone for the
  whole report.
* Do not repeat the same finding wording verbatim. Paraphrase.
* Do not invent a fifth label. If none fit, choose the closest of the
  four and explain in the summary.

Return the structured response only.
