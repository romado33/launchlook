## Quick Start Guide request (every paid tier)

After auditing {app_name}, produce a one-page Quick Start Guide for the
customer's first users. This ships as the second PDF (alongside the Main
Report) and lives at the customer's discretion: they often link it from
onboarding emails or hand it to early beta testers.

### Audience

A first-time user of {app_name}, opening it cold. They have not used
similar apps before. Plain language, no jargon, no insider references.

### Tone

Same plain-English LaunchLook voice as the audit:

* No em-dashes. No "amazing" / "seamless" / "delightful".
* No "Welcome to your journey" filler. Get to the steps.
* Imperative: "Tap Sign Up", not "You can sign up".
* Short. The full QSG fits on one printed page (5-7 steps, ~80 words each).

### Inputs

* App name: {app_name}
* App URL: {app_url}
* Builder: {builder}
* What we saw in the audit (screenshots + HTML extracts): same evidence you
  used for findings. Use it to ground the steps in real flows that appear
  to work.

### Required structure

* title: "{app_name}, Getting Started" (or close. Plain.)
* intro: 2-3 sentences. What the app is, who it's for, what this guide
  helps them do in the first five minutes.
* steps: 4-7 steps. Each has:
    - title (short, imperative: "Create your account")
    - body (1-3 sentences, concrete, references what the user actually
      sees on the page)
* footer_note: 1-2 sentences. How to get help, plus the customer's real
  support email if you can infer it from the HTML / intake. Otherwise a
  generic "Reply to your delivery email" works.

### Rules

* Only describe flows you saw working in the audit. If signup looked
  broken, do NOT include a "Sign up" step. Pivot to a flow that did work.
* Do not reference findings or the audit. The QSG is for end users, not
  for the founder. It should read as if produced by the customer.
* No filler. No "Congratulations on choosing X". Start with action.
* Same em-dash, AI-tell, and marketing-speak rules as the audit.

Return the structured response only. No preamble.
