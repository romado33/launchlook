## Handoff Report: recommended order

A real developer is going to look at this list of findings and ask
"where do I start?" Your job is to answer that question the way a senior
dev would: numbered, with a sentence of justification, no jargon.

### Findings (already drafted, with severity)

{findings_summary}

### What to produce

A short numbered list (5-10 items max) in this exact shape:

1. Fix [the most blocking thing]. [One short sentence on why this goes
   first: "the site can't take real payments until this is right" or
   "this is the only critical that affects every visitor".]
2. Then [the next thing]. [One short justification.]
3. ...

### Sequencing rules

- Anything that breaks the site for paying users beats anything cosmetic.
- Security / auth / data-exposure issues come before trust gaps come
  before broken CTAs come before copy polish.
- Group related fixes when it saves the developer a round trip. ("3. While
  you're in the Stripe dashboard, also update the webhook URL.")
- Don't recite severities by code. Lead with what the fix does.
- The list reads like an actual person prioritized it, not an algorithm.
- If there's a quick win (a 5-minute config flip) early, surface it.
- Keep each step to one or two short sentences.

### Voice rules

- Plain English.
- No em-dashes. Use colons, periods, or two sentences instead.
- No corporate language ("optimal sequence", "stakeholder alignment").
- No "Certainly", no "I recommend", no "Overall". Just the list.

Return ONLY the numbered list as plain Markdown (numbers + text). No
preamble, no JSON, no headers.
