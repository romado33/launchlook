# Cold outreach Loom script

The script that goes with every cold outreach Loom video. Adapt per prospect. Keep videos under 90 seconds.

## The script

```
Hey [name], I'm Rob — I run a small thing called Onceover that does pre-launch
checkups for vibe-coded apps.

I saw you launched [app name] on [Lovable / Bolt / Base44 / etc.] — looks
really cool. I poked at it for about 10 minutes and wanted to send you a
couple of things I noticed that you might want to fix before sharing it
more widely.

[Walk through 2-3 specific findings, with screen-share. Be friendly.
Specifically point to the actual broken or placeholder thing. Use the
visible UI label in quotes, not internal names.]

So those are the three things I'd flag. There's probably a few more small
ones — if you want, I do a Starter checkup for $9 with a fix list you can
paste into [their platform]. Link in the message I sent.

Either way, good luck with the launch — looks like a solid start.
```

## Rules of engagement

- **Always say something genuinely positive about the app.** Find something. Even a small thing. It primes the founder to be receptive.
- **Never imply the founder is incompetent.** They built something, that's brave. Findings are framed as "your AI builder forgot this," not "you missed this."
- **Never use the word "security."** That's not Onceover's wedge. Polish + placeholders + sharing risks.
- **End with "either way, good luck."** Removes pressure. Sale or not, you're a person who wished them well.
- **Don't pile on findings.** 2–3 in the Loom is the right amount. More feels harsh. The full report is for after they buy.
- **Reference the actual UI label, in quotes.** Not "your booking system" — say "your 'Book Now' button on the homepage."

## How to record

1. Open the prospect's app in Chrome (not Lovable's preview)
2. Open Loom (loom.com) → "New video" → screen + cam
3. Start the recording on your desktop showing the app
4. Audit live for ~60 seconds, narrating findings as you go
5. End with the pitch line
6. Stop, no editing
7. Loom gives you a shareable URL — paste into the DM/email

## Channels to send the Loom

| Channel | How to send |
|---------|-------------|
| Twitter/X | Quote-reply to their launch tweet OR DM if they have open DMs |
| Lovable Discord | DM (only if their profile says DMs are welcome) |
| Public reply | If their post explicitly asked for feedback, reply publicly with the Loom link |
| Email | Only if their site shows a contact email — never scraped |

**Never:** post the Loom in a community channel as a comment on someone else's post. That's spam. DM only, or reply to their own post.

## Tracking (Notion: Outreach Tracker)

After every send, log:

- Prospect name / handle
- App URL
- Channel
- Date sent
- Loom URL (so you can check view stats later)
- (Updated later) Opened, Replied, Paid

Loom shows you when someone viewed your video. Update the Opened checkbox the day you see it.

## Reply triage

| Reply type | What to do |
|------------|------------|
| "Yes, send the full report" | Drop the Stripe Payment Link for Starter. After they pay, follow normal intake flow. |
| "Interesting, what's the deal?" | One-paragraph reply: 3 tiers, what each includes, payment link. Don't oversell. |
| "Thanks but not now" | Reply with "no worries, here's the free checklist if it's useful" + link to /checklist. Move on. |
| Silence | Don't follow up. One Loom is enough. Move on. |
| Critical/data-leak finding | Always mention it explicitly, even if they don't buy. Reputation > revenue. |

## Quality bar

Before sending any Loom, confirm:

- [ ] You found at least 2 genuine, specific, fixable findings (not generic stuff)
- [ ] You said something genuinely positive about the app
- [ ] You did NOT use the words "security," "leverage," "seamless," "robust," "innovative"
- [ ] You did NOT imply the founder is unskilled
- [ ] You ended with "either way, good luck"
- [ ] Video is under 90 seconds
