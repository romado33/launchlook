Short answer

Which one fits which person

Use PageLens AI if

  • You are an SEO-focused marketer.
  • You want recurring weekly or monthly scans.
  • You live in dashboards and SEO scorecards.
  • You want a subscription that tracks site changes over time.

Use LaunchLook if

  • You shipped with Lovable, Cursor, Bolt, v0, or Replit last week.
  • You want a one-off pre-launch checkup, not a subscription.
  • You want plain-English findings with paste-ready fix prompts.
  • You want a real person reviewing every finding before it ships.

Honest trade-offs

When PageLens is the better choice

No tool fits everyone. Here is where PageLens beats LaunchLook on its strengths.

  • If you need ongoing weekly SEO monitoring, PageLens is purpose-built for that.

    LaunchLook is not a subscription, and we do not track SERP movements over time. PageLens has a Weekly Monitor for exactly that workflow.

  • If you have a marketing team that lives in dashboards, PageLens fits that workflow better.

    Recurring scorecards, trend lines, and a dashboard view are PageLens's home turf. Our deliverable is a PDF you read once and act on.

  • If you want an instant scan with no waiting, PageLens delivers in seconds.

    LaunchLook takes 24 to 48 hours because a real person reviews every finding before it ships. If you need an answer in the next 60 seconds, PageLens is faster.

Where LaunchLook fits

Why LaunchLook is different

Both tools share a category. The audience is different, and so is the deliverable.

Built for the vibe coder, not the marketing team. Our buyer launched a site with Lovable, Cursor, or Bolt last week and wants to know "would I be embarrassed if a stranger opened this link today?" That is a different question from "how does my site rank for keyword X."

Every finding has a paste-ready fix prompt. You copy the prompt, drop it back into your AI builder, and the fix lands. No translation step, no Google-the-error-code step. That is the entire point.

We catch the embarrassing visible stuff, not just SEO scores. Broken buttons and dead links. Exposed keys on the live URL. Sketchy footer text that still says "Your Company Name." Placeholder copy left over from the template. SEO scorecards do not surface these.

One-off pricing, no monthly lock-in. $19 Starter, $49 Scale Up, $99 Pro. You pay once, you get a checkup, you move on. We do not auto-renew, and we do not charge you to look at your site again next month.

Questions

Comparison FAQ

Should I use both?+

Yes, they solve different problems. PageLens is purpose-built for ongoing SEO monitoring (recurring scans, dashboards, trend tracking). LaunchLook is purpose-built for the one-time pre-launch checkup (broken CTAs, placeholder copy, trust gaps, mobile breakage, plus paste-ready fix prompts you drop into your AI builder). Most buyers use one or the other based on which job they need done; some use both at different points in the lifecycle.

Why isn't this comparison in your main navigation?+

Because most of our customers don't know PageLens. This page is for the ones who do. We would rather earn buyers on our own strengths than by knocking competitors. The page exists for SEO and for the FAQ. It does not live in our main nav, our hero, or our pricing cards.

How fast can I get a report from LaunchLook?+

24 to 48 hours, with a real human (Rob, the founder) reviewing every finding before delivery. Most reports go out in 24. PageLens is faster (instant), but that is because there is no human review. The trade-off is real, and we are explicit about it.

Is this comparison sponsored or biased?+

No sponsorship. We wrote this because buyers occasionally ask us "how is LaunchLook different from PageLens?" and we wanted one canonical answer. Pricing and feature details for PageLens were summarized from their public site as of May 2026 and may change. If anything here is wrong or out of date, email hello@launchlook.app and we will fix it.

What does "vibe coder" mean here?+

Someone who built a working web app using an AI builder like Lovable, Bolt, Base44, Replit, v0, or Cursor, often without a deep coding background. The site works. They want to know if it is ready for real users. That is who LaunchLook is built for.
