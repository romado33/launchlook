LaunchLook vs. PageLens AI

Both start with a URL. PageLens turns a public website into a fast, scored launch-readiness report (SEO, accessibility, headers, mobile, trust signals, health score, Markdown for agents). LaunchLook is a founder-reviewed checkup for vibe-coded apps: someone actually submits your forms, checks whether emails arrive, and edits the findings before you get a PDF with paste-into-builder fix text for Lovable, Bolt, or Cursor.

PageLens is strong on “is my site indexable and technically clean?” LaunchLook is strong on “will a real person sign up, trust this, and not hit a broken mobile layout?” Many founders use a scanner first, then LaunchLook before they share the link.

Honest trade-offs

When PageLens is the better choice

Choose PageLens when you want a fast website scorecard: health score, noindex/schema/header issues, accessibility signals, many public pages at once, Markdown you can hand to Cursor, or a cheap re-scan after fixes. It is built for speed and breadth on the public surface.

Choose LaunchLook when you are shipping an app people log into and you care whether signup actually works, emails arrive, mobile layout breaks in real use, or users could see each other’s data. We are slower because a founder clicks through and edits the list before it ships.

Many teams do both: PageLens for indexing and technical readiness, LaunchLook before they send the link to beta users or investors. We are not trying to replace PageLens on SEO; we cover the layer scanners skip.
