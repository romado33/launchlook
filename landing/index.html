What do you actually test?

We walk your live URL like a first-time visitor — desktop and phone. Typical checks include:

  • Signup and login. We register (or use a test account) and check whether confirmation and password-reset emails actually arrive.
  • Forms and checkout. Contact, booking, and payment flows — we submit them and see if anything breaks or fails silently. That’s the #1 thing we find.
  • Mobile layout. Your URL at phone width: tap targets, menus, text spilling off-screen, CTAs that disappear.
  • Trust pages. Privacy policy, terms, contact or support links — do they exist and work?
  • Dead links and buttons. Clicks that go nowhere, 404s, or error screens a visitor would hit.
  • Placeholder and dev-only content. Lorem ipsum, fake ratings, seed data, “Dev Bypass” still showing on the live site.
  • First-visitor paths. Can someone get from your homepage to the thing you’re selling without getting stuck?

What AI builders can’t test

Five things your AI builder can’t catch

Your builder won’t catch these. We see them on almost every app we review.

1. Silent form failures

You hit submit. Nothing happens. No email, no error. The UI looks fine; nobody tested if it actually sends. #1 thing we find.

2. Mobile layout problems

Buttons you can’t tap, text spilling off the screen, CTAs hidden behind the menu. Desktop preview lies. Your phone doesn’t.

3. Dev junk still live

“Dev Bypass” login, seed data, fake team photos, ratings that say 4.5 (0). Builders ship this stuff unless you catch it.

4. Missing trust pages

No privacy policy, no terms, contact links that go nowhere. People check this before they pay. Then they leave.

5. Copy that sounds robotic

Vague hero lines, filler words, that “written by AI” vibe. People feel it even if they can’t explain it. They leave.

Questions

Common questions

What do you actually test?+

We walk your live URL like a first-time visitor — desktop and phone. Typical checks:

  • Signup and login — confirmation emails actually arrive
  • Forms and checkout — silent failures are the #1 thing we find
  • Mobile layout — phone width, tap targets, menus, CTAs
  • Trust pages — privacy, terms, working contact links
  • Dead links and buttons — 404s and dead-end clicks
  • Placeholder and dev-only content — fake stats, dev shortcuts on prod
  • First-visitor paths — homepage to core action without getting stuck
What do you mean by “we actually use it”?+

We click through your app. Signup, contact forms, checkout if you have it. We submit stuff and check if confirmation emails show up. That’s how we catch “the form looked fine but nothing ever sent.”

A person edits the list. Tools help us spot things faster. Before you see anything, junk gets cut and wording gets cleaned up.

You get a short list from a real walkthrough, not a wall of automated warnings.

How is this different from a security scanner?+

We focus on what visitors see: broken UI, placeholders, trust pages. For deep security, use something like VAS. Use both if you can.

Which platforms do you support?+

Any vibe-coded web app with a public URL. We often review Lovable, Bolt, Base44, and Replit apps. Also v0, Cursor-built apps, Webflow sites, and others. If your builder outputs a shareable link, we can usually review it.

How is this different from VibeDoctor or PageLens?+

VibeDoctor needs a GitHub repo for most checks (secrets, CVEs, code health). PageLens is a fast site scanner (SEO, schema, headers). LaunchLook only needs your live URL and focuses on whether a real person can sign up, get email, and use the app on a phone.

I built my app with Lovable / Cursor / Bolt. Why do I still need a review?+

Your builder is built to ship fast. It doesn’t pretend to be a confused first-time user, wait for signup emails, or catch the button that dies on a phone.

We do. Someone who’s never seen your app tries to use it the way you intended. Most apps we review have 5 to 15 things a first visitor would notice before you do.

Lovable’s SEO check helps Google. We help the friend you just texted the link to: dead buttons, forms that fail quietly, mobile layouts that break, “Dev Bypass” still on the login page.

My app is still on a preview URL. Can you review it now?+

Yes. Lovable preview URLs, Vercel staging URLs, and any publicly reachable URL work. We flag anything that will need to be different on your custom domain (like a missing canonical or noindex setting). localhost won’t work.

I’m worried you’ll steal my idea or pass it off as your own. How do I know you won’t?+

That’s a fair thing to worry about, and I want to be direct about it.

We only see what anyone on the internet can see. Your live public URL. Same as a random visitor. No source code, no Lovable login, no GitHub. If it’s not public yet, we can’t see it.

One person reviews your audit: me (Rob), the founder. Not a team, not outsourced. Screenshots get deleted after your report goes out.

I’m a technical writer, not a dev trying to clone your app. I couldn’t rebuild what you built if I tried, and I don’t want to. One bad story in the Discord and I’m done. So yeah, I take this seriously.
