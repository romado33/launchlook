Pre-launch checklist for vibe-coded apps

A short list for founders about to share a real link, built with Lovable, Bolt, Base44, Replit, v0, Cursor, or similar.

Open your app in a normal browser tab (not the builder preview). Check what you can. Fix what you can. Get help for the rest.

Want the longer version? Full checklist on GitHub (optional deep checks).

How to use it

  1. Work through each section, about 20 minutes total.
  2. Mark each line: done, problem, or not sure.
  3. Problems become your fix list. “Not sure” is a good reason to get a second pair of eyes.

This is what visitors notice. Not a code review or security audit. For deep security, tools like VAS complement this well.

1. First look

~5 minutes · homepage and footer

  • The live link loads without feeling broken or endlessly spinning
  • The browser tab shows your app name (not “My App” or generic builder text)
  • A stranger can tell what your app does within a few seconds of reading the homepage
  • No placeholder text left behind (lorem ipsum, “Your Company Name,” “[insert tagline],” obvious TODO notes)
  • No default “welcome to your new app” copy from the builder (it sounds like your product)
  • Your product name appears in the header, not the platform’s logo as the star
  • Nothing obviously “for developers only” on the public site (test data buttons, skip-login, seed sample data)
  • The main button on the homepage does what it promises when you click it

2. Trust & contact

~3 minutes

  • Privacy policy page works (often at /privacy)
  • Terms page works (often at /terms)
  • Visitors can find how to reach you (support email in footer or a contact page)
  • That email is real and one you check, not support@example.com
  • Footer links to privacy and terms work (no “page not found”)

3. Does the main flow work?

~10 minutes · the one thing users come to do

  • Buttons and menu links you tried actually do something (no dead ends)
  • If people sign up: confirmation email arrives (check spam)
  • If people sign up: you can log in afterward with that account
  • If people sign up: you can log out
  • The core workflow finishes: book, pay, submit, save, whatever your app is for
  • After submitting a form, you see a clear “it worked” or “something went wrong” message
  • If you charge money: a test payment completes and shows a sensible thank-you page
  • A brand-new user isn’t staring at a totally blank screen with no hint what to do next

4. On a phone

~5 minutes · open the site on your phone or narrow the browser window

  • No sideways scrolling. Everything fits the screen width
  • Text is readable without pinching to zoom
  • Buttons are easy to tap (not tiny)
  • The main workflow is usable on phone, not only on desktop

5. Sharing your link

~2 minutes

  • Paste your URL into Slack, iMessage, or LinkedIn: the preview shows the right title and description (not generic builder fluff)

6. If people can sign in (optional)

Skip if your app has no accounts · otherwise create two throwaway test accounts

  • Signed in as person A, you cannot see person B’s private stuff (messages, files, bookings, etc.)
  • When signed out, opening “dashboard” or “settings” asks you to log in. It doesn’t show someone else’s data.

7. Gut check

~2 minutes

  • You’d be comfortable sending this link to a friend today
  • You wouldn’t be embarrassed if someone screenshotted the homepage

Beyond the App — Launch Infrastructure

12 categories · the operational plumbing around your app

The 38 items above are about your app itself. These are about everything around the app — the operational plumbing that turns “I built something cool” into “real customers can actually buy it.” Vibe-coded founders typically overlook most of this. None of it is technically hard; it’s just easy to forget when you’ve been heads-down building.

Payments

  • Do you have a way to take payments?
  • Are your payment links tested end-to-end (you’ve made at least one real test purchase)?
  • Does the customer receive a receipt after paying?
  • Do you have a documented refund process you’d be willing to send to a customer?

Examples: Stripe, Lemon Squeezy, Paddle, Gumroad, Polar.

Custom domain

  • Is your app on your own domain (not the builder’s *.lovable.app / *.bolt.new / *.vercel.app subdomain)?
  • Do both www.yourdomain.com and yourdomain.com work?
  • Does HTTPS work without certificate warnings?

Examples: Cloudflare, Namecheap, Porkbun for DNS.

Outbound email (transactional)

  • Can your app actually send transactional emails (password resets, confirmations, receipts)?
  • Are your emails landing in the inbox, not spam? (Send to yourself at Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo to check.)
  • Is the From address on your own domain (not noreply@gmail.com or a generic provider)?

Examples: Resend, Postmark, SendGrid, AWS SES, Loops.

Inbound email

  • If a customer emails hello@yourdomain.com, does it actually reach your real inbox?
  • Have you tested this from a separate email account (not the same one your forwarding sends to)?

Examples: ImprovMX (free forwarding), Fastmail, Google Workspace, Zoho Mail.

Customer tracking

  • Do you have a single place that stores who paid, who signed up, and who churned?
  • Can you find a specific customer’s record in under 30 seconds?
  • Can you tie a payment, an intake response, and a support email back to the same customer?

Examples: Notion, Airtable, a simple spreadsheet, HubSpot Free, Pipedrive.

Intake and forms

  • If a customer needs to send you info (intake, support, feedback), do you have a form ready?
  • Are submissions saved somewhere and do you get notified when one arrives?

Examples: Tally, Typeform, Google Forms, Formspree.

Analytics

  • Do you know how many people visit your app each day?
  • Do you know where they come from (referrer, campaign, channel)?
  • Do you know what they click and where they drop off?

Examples: Plausible, Umami, Fathom, GA4.

Backups

  • If your builder went down or deleted your project tomorrow, do you have a copy of your code and data?
  • When did you last verify the backup actually works (not just exists)?

Examples: GitHub repo (most builders support this), periodic code export, deployed staging copy.

Legal pages

  • Do /privacy and /terms exist and load (not 404)?
  • Are they linked from the footer on every page?
  • Do they accurately describe what data you actually collect and what you do with it?
  • Does your refund policy match what your payment provider expects?

Examples: Termly, Iubenda, manually drafted (with a “not legal advice” disclaimer).

Branding

  • Do you have a custom logo (not the builder’s default placeholder)?
  • Do you have a custom favicon (not the builder’s default)?
  • Do you have an Open Graph image so links to your app preview nicely in iMessage, Slack, and LinkedIn?

Examples: Canva, Figma, a small commission for the logo.

Customer support

  • Where do customer complaints and refund requests actually land?
  • Do you have a documented response time you can realistically hit?

Examples: Personal email, Help Scout, Crisp, Intercom — start with a personal email and upgrade later.

Status and incident communication

  • If your app breaks, can customers tell something is wrong (vs. silently failing)?
  • Do they have a way to report it that doesn’t require digging through your site for an email?

Examples: Footer status link, a simple status page, a clearly-visible support email.

What to do next

  • Fix before you share widely: broken signup, missing privacy/terms, placeholder text, dev-only buttons still visible, one user seeing another’s data.
  • Fix soon after launch: mobile layout quirks, weak link previews, empty screens without guidance.
  • More than ~5 serious problems? Pause the launch push until the worst ones are handled.
