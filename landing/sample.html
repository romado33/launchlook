🔴 Critical

Dev login bypass visible to everyone

What we saw: On /auth, a "Dev Bypass (Skip Login)" button opens a role picker and signs visitors in without credentials. Helper text reads "For development purposes only."

Why it matters: Anyone with your URL can skip authentication on a marketplace app.

Copy-paste fix prompt for Lovable

Remove the "Dev Bypass (Skip Login)" button, the "Choose Your Dev Role" modal, and all "For development purposes only" copy from /auth. Only show these when import.meta.env.DEV is true. Never on the published lovable.app URL.

Developer Tools on the homepage

What we saw: After sign-in, the homepage shows "Developer Tools" with "Seed Sample Data" and "Clear My Data" buttons.

Why it matters: Real users will think the product is unfinished.

Copy-paste fix prompt for Lovable

Remove the "Developer Tools" / "Development Data Seeder" section from the published homepage. Gate the Seed and Clear data buttons behind import.meta.env.DEV only.

🟠 High

Missing /privacy and /terms (404)

What we saw: Both /privacy and /terms return the custom 404 page.

Why it matters: The app collects booking and account data. Visitors and payment tools expect legal pages.

Copy-paste fix prompt for Lovable

Add /privacy and /terms routes with basic policy content. Link both from the footer on every page. Use my real support email in the contact section. Match the rest of the site's typography and spacing.

Every listing says "Hosted by Local Host"

What we saw: Every experience card shows "Hosted by Local Host" instead of a real host name.

Why it matters: Reads as test data on a public marketplace. First-time visitors will not trust the listings.

Quick Book dead-ends with no slots

What we saw: "Quick Book" opens a modal, but the time-slot picker shows "No available slots found" and the "Book Now" button stays disabled.

Why it matters: The single most prominent CTA on the marketplace dead-ends. Visitors who try to book bounce.

🟡 Medium

Cards show 4.5 stars with (0) reviews

What we saw: Listing cards display "4.5 (0)" (a numeric rating with zero reviews).

Why it matters: Looks like fake or seeded data, which kills trust on a marketplace.
